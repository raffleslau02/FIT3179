Life Expectancy Insights: A Multi-Chart Analysis for the Year 2014

Life Expectancy, a crucial metric in public health, gauges the average lifespan of a population. It provides invaluable insights into a nation's overall well-being and healthcare system. Explore the factors influencing global life expectancy in my visualizations, revealing the complex interplay of demographics, healthcare, and socioeconomic conditions.

The map visualisation shows the life expectancy of each country in 2014. Darker shades represent countries with higher life expectancy, while lighter shades indicate lower life expectancy. This map offers an overview of how life expectancy is distributed globally, highlighting areas where people tend to live longer or face challenges to their life expectancy.

Further insights on the top-performing countries can be explored in the accompanying chart.


This bar chart shows the top 5 countries with the highest life expectancy in 2014 and their Human Development Index (HDI) in terms of income composition of resources. From this chart, Germany can be seen to have the highest HDI, at 0.92.

These countries exhibit exceptional life expectancy, reflecting strong healthcare and societal well-being. The HDI offers more insights, indicating a well-rounded approach to development, where income distribution plays a significant role. It sets as an example for other countries looking to improve their citizens' well-being.


This pie chart visually represents the distribution of countries into two main categories: 'developing' and 'developed.' The size of each slice reflects the proportion of countries in each category. The chart provides a clear overview of the global distribution of nations based on their socioeconomic development status. It serves as a valuable tool for understanding the balance between developing and developed countries, highlighting the diversity in economic and social progress worldwide.

From the pie chart, most countries can be seen to still be developing while only a small fraction, 17.49% of the countries are already developed in 2014.


This interactive bubble plot offers a comprehensive view of the relationship between life expectancy and total government expenditure on healthcare as a percentage of total government expenditure (%). Each bubble represents a specific country, with the size of the bubble indicating its population size. The color of each bubble distinguishes between 'developing' and 'developed' countries, providing valuable context.

Germany can be seen to have a high total expenditure(%) of 11.3% whereas Belgium has a low total expenditure(%), of only 1.59%. Interestingly, both countries share the same life expectancy.

One key observation is the wide variation in life expectancy and healthcare expenditure across countries. Bubbles higher up have longer life expectancies, and those to the right spend more on healthcare. This reveals that some countries achieve longer life expectancies with less spending, while others require more investments to attain similar results.

From the correlation heat map, Diphtheria and Polio have a positive correlation with life expectancy. This suggests that in countries with high vaccination rates for these diseases, life expectancy tends to be higher.

Adult Mortality has a strong negative correlation with life expectancy which indicates that countries where adult mortality is high, life expectancy tends to be lower.

The heatmap shows how these health factors are connected, revealing that public health is a complex and multifaceted issue.
