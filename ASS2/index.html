Life Expectancy Insights: A Multi-Chart Analysis for the Year 2014

Life Expectancy, a crucial metric in public health, gauges the average lifespan of a population. It provides invaluable insights into a nation's overall well-being and healthcare system. Explore the factors influencing global life expectancy in my visualizations, revealing the complex interplay of demographics, healthcare, and socioeconomic conditions.

The map visualisation shows the life expectancy of each country in 2014. Darker shades represent countries with higher life expectancy, while lighter shades indicate lower life expectancy. This map offers an overview of how life expectancy is distributed globally, highlighting areas where people tend to live longer or face challenges to their life expectancy.
Further insights on the top-performing countries can be explored in the accompanying chart.


This bar chart shows the top 5 countries with the highest life expectancy in 2014 and their Human Development Index (HDI) in terms of income composition of resources. From this chart, Germany can be seen to have the highest HDI, at 0.92.
These countries exhibit exceptional life expectancy, reflecting strong healthcare and societal well-being. The HDI offers more insights, indicating a well-rounded approach to development, where income distribution plays a significant role. It sets as an example for other countries looking to improve their citizens' well-being.
